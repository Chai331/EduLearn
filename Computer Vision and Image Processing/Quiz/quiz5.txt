1. In computer vision, what is an edge?
A. A uniform region of constant intensity
B. A significant local change or discontinuity in image intensity
C. The center point of a digital image
D. The lowest intensity pixel in an image
Answer: B

2. Which type of edge profile represents an instantaneous and abrupt change in intensity?
A. Step edge
B. Ramp edge
C. Roof edge
D. Line edge
Answer: A

3. In real images, ideal step edges become ramp edges due to:
A. High resolution sensors
B. Image acquisition imperfection and illumination conditions
C. Highpass filtering
D. Thresholding
Answer: B

4. The three main steps in edge detection algorithms are Filtering, Enhancement, and:
A. Acquisition
B. Digitization
C. Detection
D. Compression
Answer: C

5. Which property of the first-order derivative is used to detect the presence of an edge?
A. Sign
B. Magnitude
C. Zero-crossing
D. Phase
Answer: B

6. In a second-order derivative, what feature indicates the midpoint of a ramp edge?
A. Local maximum
B. Local minimum
C. Zero-crossing
D. Infinite peak
Answer: C

7. Which operator is the 2D equivalent of the second derivative?
A. Gradient
B. Sobel
C. Laplacian
D. Prewitt
Answer: C

8. Why is the standard Laplacian operator not frequently used alone for edge detection?
A. It is too slow to compute.
B. It is highly sensitive to noise.
C. It only detects horizontal edges.
D. It produces a blurred output.
Answer: B

9. To combat noise sensitivity, the Laplacian of Gaussian (LoG) operator combines the Laplacian with which type of filter?
A. Highpass filter
B. Median filter
C. Gaussian smoothing filter
D. Contrast stretching
Answer: C

10. The Laplacian of Gaussian (LoG) operator is commonly known as the:
A. Top-hat operator
B. Mexican hat operator
C. Bell curve operator
D. Step operator
Answer: B
