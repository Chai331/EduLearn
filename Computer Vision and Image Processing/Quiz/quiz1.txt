1. What is the primary goal of Computer Vision?
A. To enhance or modify images at a pixel level
B. To extract meaning and understanding from images
C. To produce an image from a 3D model
D. To compress image files for storage
Answer: B

2. Which of the following is an example of an application of Computer Vision?
A. Noise removal
B. Image compression
C. 3D reconstruction from 2D images
D. Contrast enhancement
Answer: C

3. How does Computer Vision differ from Computer Graphics?
A. Computer Vision produces images from 3D models.
B. Computer Graphics reconstructs 3D worlds from images.
C. Computer Vision is the inverse of Computer Graphics.
D. Both have the exact same goals and outputs.
Answer: C

4. Which of the following is NOT a typical component of a computer vision system?
A. Camera
B. Lighting
C. Computer
D. Audio synthesizer
Answer: D

5. What was the "ridiculously brief history" milestone for computer vision in 1966?
A. First autonomous vehicle was built.
B. Minsky assigned it as an undergraduate summer project.
C. The first digital camera was invented.
D. Facial recognition was perfected.
Answer: B

6. What does OCR stand for in the context of computer vision?
A. Optical Character Recognition
B. Object Color Rendering
C. Optimal Contrast Ratio
D. Optical Camera Resolution
Answer: A

7. Which of the following fields is strongly tied to Computer Vision to enable decision-making?
A. Artificial Intelligence
B. Database Management
C. Operating Systems
D. Web Development
Answer: A

8. What is the first step in the Computer Vision process?
A. Segmentation
B. Preprocessing
C. Image Acquisition
D. Recognition & Interpretation
Answer: C

9. What is the key function of the preprocessing stage in computer vision?
A. To assign meaning to an object
B. To digitize the analog signal
C. To improve the image to increase the chances of success for later processes
D. To extract individual characters
Answer: C

10. Which stage partitions an input image into its constituent parts or objects?
A. Image Acquisition
B. Preprocessing
C. Segmentation
D. Description
Answer: C
