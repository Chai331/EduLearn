1. What is the smallest unit of a digital image?
A. Voxel
B. Pixel
C. Bit
D. Channel
Answer: B

2. In an 8-bit grayscale image, what is the intensity range?
A. 0 to 1
B. 0 to 255
C. 1 to 256
D. 0 to 16.7 million
Answer: B

3. How many bits per channel are typically used in a 24-bit true color image?
A. 3 bits
B. 8 bits
C. 16 bits
D. 24 bits
Answer: B

4. The human eye is sensitive to radiation with wavelengths ranging from:
A. 100nm to 300nm
B. 400nm to 700nm
C. 800nm to 1000nm
D. 1mm to 10mm
Answer: B

5. Which part of the human eye controls the amount of light passing through the lens?
A. Retina
B. Fovea
C. Iris
D. Cornea
Answer: C

6. Which area of the retina has a very dense population of color receptors called cones?
A. Iris
B. Pupil
C. Fovea
D. Lens
Answer: C

7. In the pinhole model, where does the imaging system's center of projection coincide?
A. The focal length
B. The origin of the 3-D coordinate system
C. The image plane
D. The line of sight
Answer: B

8. The distance between the focal center of the lens and the retina in the human eye varies approximately between:
A. 10 mm to 12 mm
B. 14 mm to 17 mm
C. 20 mm to 25 mm
D. 50 mm to 100 mm
Answer: B

9. Which technology is a flexible and common input device for machine vision systems that converts light into electrical charge?
A. CMOS
B. CCD
C. CRT
D. LCD
Answer: B

10. Compared to CCD sensors, CMOS sensors traditionally:
A. Consume much more power
B. Have higher light sensitivity
C. Are more susceptible to noise
D. Cannot be read individually per pixel
Answer: C
