1. What is the main objective of image enhancement?
A. To compress the image size
B. To transform an image into a form more suitable for a specific application
C. To extract 3D information from 2D images
D. To classify objects in an image
Answer: B

2. Which category of image enhancement is based on direct manipulation of pixels in the image plane?
A. Frequency domain methods
B. Spatial domain methods
C. Temporal domain methods
D. Transform domain methods
Answer: B

3. Which of the following is considered a point operation in spatial domain image enhancement?
A. Neighborhood averaging
B. Median filtering
C. Contrast stretching
D. Spatial lowpass filtering
Answer: C

4. A spatial domain transformation that produces a two-level (binary) image is called:
A. Contrast stretching
B. Bit-plane slicing
C. Thresholding
D. Image negative
Answer: C

5. What does the x-axis of an image intensity histogram represent?
A. Frequency of pixel intensities
B. Spatial coordinates of pixels
C. Gray level intensities
D. Number of color channels
Answer: C

6. In a normalized histogram, what does the sum of all components equal?
A. 0
B. 1
C. 255
D. Total number of pixels
Answer: B

7. A dark image typically has a histogram that is concentrated on the:
A. Left side (lower intensity values)
B. Right side (higher intensity values)
C. Middle
D. Entire range uniformly
Answer: A

8. What type of image enhancement technique increases the dynamic range of gray levels in the image?
A. Thresholding
B. Contrast stretching
C. Bit-plane slicing
D. Lowpass filtering
Answer: B

9. Which operation is useful for distinguishing pixels of a higher intensity from pixels with a lower intensity value, separating foreground from background?
A. Thresholding
B. Averaging
C. Image negatives
D. Integration
Answer: A

10. In adaptive thresholding, the threshold value T depends on:
A. A single global constant
B. Spatial coordinates or local neighborhood properties
C. The image's frequency domain
D. The bit-plane level
Answer: B
